Matt's Cheesecake

Home ‚Üí Desserts ‚Üí Matt's Cheesecake

Ingredients

Cheesecake Batter

  • Four 8-ounce packages of Philadelphia cream cheese
  • Five large eggs
  • Two cups sour cream
  • One stick (8 tablespoons) butter
  • One and a half cups granulated sugar
  • Two tablespoons cornstarch (or flour)*
  • One and a half teaspoons vanilla extract
  • One teaspoon apple cider vinegar (or lemon juice)

Graham Cracker Crust

  • Fourteen full sheets of graham crackers, crumbed
  • One stick (8 tablespoons) butter, melted

Instructions

Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 300¬∞F.
  2. To prevent a soggy crust, place a ten-inch spring-form pan into an oven cooking bag and press the sides of the oven safe bag around the outside edges of the spring-form pan.
  3. Tightly wrap 2 layers of aluminum foil around the outside of the spring-form pan covering the oven bag, the bottom of the pan, and the sides of the pan.
  4. Generously coat inside of the pan with butter or nonstick spray.
  5. Use a food processor or blender to grind the graham cracker sheets into little pieces. Alternatively you can crush them with a rolling pin (in a zip bag) or by hand/with a knife.
  6. Mix the graham cracker crumbs and the melted butter together until combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of a spring form pan and slightly up the sides. To make sure it is tight and compact, use the bottom and sides of a measuring cup to press the crust into the bottom and sides of the pan.
  7. Pre-bake the crust for about 10 minutes at 300¬∞F.

Cheesecake Batter

  1. Using an electric mixer (ideally with a paddle attachment), beat the cream cheese and butter together until smooth.
  2. Add in the eggs, one at a time, and beat until they are fully blended.
  3. Add in the sour cream and mix until smooth.
  4. Add in the sugar, cornstarch (or flour), vanilla and apple cider vinegar (or lemon juice), and beat for about two minutes until completely smooth. Be careful here to NOT overmix your batter.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared and already baked graham cacker crust (in the spring-form pan).
  6. Place the filled pan (the cheesecake) into a bain marie (water bath; I usually use a large roasting pan).
  7. Place the roasting pan, spring-form pan, and cheesecake (i.e., all nestled inside one another) into the oven and carefully pour water (ideally boiling) into the roasting pan to reach about halfway up the sides of the spring-form pan. (Be sure to avoid splashing any water onto the cheesecake!).
  8. Bake for about 2 hours, or until the cake is lightly browned on top. Once done, remove the roasting pan from the oven and let the spring-from pan/cheesecake sit in the warm water for a few minutes (15-20 minutes is what I usually do).
  9. Carefully remove the spring-form pan from the water bath (bain marie). Then, remove the foil and oven safe bag from around the spring-form pan. Let the cheesecake cool at room temperature for about half an hour (I usually put it on a cooling rack to let it cool a bit).
  10. After the springform pan has cooled enough to be able to handle (about half an hour), undo the aluminum foil and cooking bag (and anything else aroudn the spring-form pan) and let the cheesecake cool on the counter (or cooling rack) for another half hour.
  11. Finally, after the cake has cooled for about an hour on the counter, while still in the spring-form pan, cover and fully chill the cheesecake (ideally leave the cheesecake in the fridge overnight).
  12. When ready to serve, run a knife (or offset spatula) around the rim of the spinrgform pan between the sides of the pan and the cheesecake, and then unlatch the pan. Remove the rim of the springform pan (carefully), decorate (if desired; e.g., with whipped cream and other toppings), and serve (preferably chilled).

Notes

  • Room Temp: For the best texture, all ingredients (but especially those kept in the fridge) should be room temperature before mixing. If not, you might end up with globs of cream cheese that are not fully incorporated. That said, even if this happens it will still generally be fine.
  • *Starch (or Flour): Starch is added to the ingredient list to ensure that the cheesecake will set properly after baking. While it can be omitted (for a lovely texture), you do run the risk of the cake not fully setting. I have also reduced the amount or intentionally left it out depending on other starch-like ingredients (e.g., cocoa powder) added to the recipe (e.g., for a chocolate variation).
